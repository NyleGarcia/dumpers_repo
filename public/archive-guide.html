What is Dumper's Repo?

Dumper's Repo is a comprehensive toolkit for Star Citizen players who want to engage with the game's crafting and economy systems without getting ripped off.

Whether you're tracking which blueprints you've unlocked, managing your mined resources, coordinating crafting orders with your org, or just trying to figure out what a fair price is for that pile of Quantanium you just refined — Dumper's Repo has you covered.

The site is designed to be a one-stop shop for crafters, miners, and traders who want transparency and fairness in their in-game economic activities.

Data Migration

Offline progress is stored in your browser using the same IDs as member accounts. Old offline data from before a recent update is cleared automatically when you visit. On your first sign-in (when the welcome onboarding appears), valid offline data migrates to your account — unmatched items are skipped. If you already have an account, your offline stash stays separate in the browser.

Offline data is stored in your browser. It persists across sessions but won't sync between devices or browsers until you create an account.

Why Dumper's Fair-Value Price (DFP)?

The Problem

"Grey market" trading sites are plagued with price gouging. People asking 5 billion aUEC for items that take maybe an hour to acquire yourself. It's predatory, it's frustrating, and CIG/RSI rightfully despises these practices.

The Solution

Dumper's Fair-Value Price (DFP) is an algorithmic pricing system that calculates what resources and crafted items are actually worth based on:

  • Time investment required to acquire/craft
  • Resource rarity and availability
  • Quality tier (500-1000 scale, with exponential value curves)
  • Blueprint acquisition difficulty and reputation requirements

The Goal

Create a pricing standard the community can rally behind. When everyone uses DFP, buyers know they're getting fair deals, sellers know they're being compensated fairly, and the exploitative grey market loses its power.

Buyer & Fulfiller Ratings

The Custom Orders and Fulfillment system includes a reputation rating system to help build trust between buyers and fulfillers.

As a Buyer

  • Create orders specifying what you need
  • Rate fulfillers after delivery (1-5 stars)
  • Your ratings help others choose reliable fulfillers

As a Fulfiller

  • Claim and complete orders to build reputation
  • Your average rating is visible to buyers
  • Higher ratings = more trust from the community

Note: Both buyers and fulfillers must have a verified RSI Handle to participate in the order system. This ensures accountability and helps prevent scams.

Building Your Reputation

New members start with "Pending" reputation until they complete 5 successful orders (as either buyer or fulfiller). During this time, there are some limits to help protect the community:

Pending Buyer Limits

  • Maximum of 2 active orders at a time
  • Total order value capped at 1,000,000 aUEC
  • Limits are lifted after 5 completed orders

Pending Fulfiller Limits

  • Can only accept 1 order at a time
  • Complete it before accepting another
  • Limits are lifted after 5 completed fulfillments

Important: Everyone must rate their completed orders before creating or accepting new ones. This keeps the rating system fair and encourages timely feedback.

Order System Rules & Expectations

The order system is built on trust and fairness. To protect all members, we enforce the following rules — especially for users still building their reputation.

✓ What's Expected

  • Create orders for items/resources you genuinely need
  • Complete transactions in good faith
  • Rate orders promptly after completion
  • Communicate clearly with your buyer/fulfiller
  • Use your verified RSI Handle for all in-game trades

✗ What's Not Allowed

  • Creating duplicate orders for the same item while one is being fulfilled
  • Making artificially small orders to farm reputation quickly
  • Repeatedly trading with the same person to inflate ratings
  • Using multiple accounts to manipulate the order system
  • Cancelling orders without good reason to waste fulfillers' time
  • Refusing to rate completed orders

⚠ Pending Rep Requirements

  • Minimum order value: 10,000 aUEC per order
  • No duplicate orders: Cannot create another order for the same blueprint if one is pending or being fulfilled
  • Order limits: Max 2 active orders / 1M aUEC total as buyer, 1 order at a time as fulfiller

⏱ Time Limits

  • Fulfiller deadline: 72 hours to mark an accepted order ready, or it releases back to the pool
  • Buyer pickup: 72 hours to confirm pickup after ready, or the order auto-completes (buyer may receive a strike)
  • Rating deadline: 24 hours after the other party rates, or a 5-star rating is auto-applied on your behalf
  • 3 strikes in 30 days triggers an automatic report to officers

Consequences for Violations

  • Reputation reset: All ratings cleared, returning you to "Pending" status with limits
  • Order history cleared: Archived orders may be removed along with your reputation
  • Account ban: Severe or repeated violations may result in permanent removal from the platform

Suspicious activity is automatically detected and reported to site staff for review.

Protecting Yourself in Trades

In-game trades happen outside the site. Keep your own records so disputes can be resolved fairly.

  • Screenshot aUEC transfers before and after handoff
  • Record video of the exchange when possible
  • Note the other party's RSI Handle, location, and time
  • Keep Spectrum or in-game chat logs
  • If a fulfiller marked ready but you didn't receive goods, use Report Problem on the order — do not wait for the 72-hour auto-complete

Evidence is not uploaded on the site. If officers need proof, they may ask you to email screenshots or share a cloud storage link (Google Drive, Imgur, etc.).

Mining Guide Reference

The Mining Guide provides comprehensive data about ore locations throughout the Star Citizen universe.

Features

  • View by Ore: See all locations where a specific ore can be found
  • View by Location: See all ores available at a specific mining site
  • Rarity Tiers: Ores are categorized by rarity from Common to Legendary
  • RS Signatures: Each mineable ore has a unique Resource Signature value
  • Track Button: Add ores to your Mining Tracker (signature-only ores)

Understanding RS (Resource Signatures)

When scanning asteroids or surface deposits, your ship scanner shows a cluster RS value. This value equals the number of nodes × the ore's base RS signature. Use the Mining Tracker to quickly reference signature multiples while scanning.

Note: Hand-mineables (gems) and ground vehicle mineables do not have traditional RS signatures and cannot be tracked in the Mining Tracker.

Factions Reference

Each faction tracks your reputation separately. Completing contracts and missions increases your standing, unlocking access to higher-tier contracts and blueprint rewards.

Note: Different factions use different rank names — Covalex uses "Trainee → Master" while Bounty Hunters Guild uses "Applicant → Guild Steward". Some factions also have multiple career tracks (e.g., Bounty Hunting vs Security).

Reputation Progression

All factions follow a similar progression pattern from neutral/hostile through various tiers to their highest rank. Higher standings unlock:

  • Better-paying contracts
  • Exclusive blueprint rewards
  • Access to restricted areas or services
